alok · a story

i don't really know why you're here… but since you are, this is me.

raw thoughts. no filter. no resume.
just the actual thing.

scroll
i wasn't some special kid or anything. i was the kind of kid who was always a little scared — scared that if i don't do this, or say that, people won't want me around. it sounds dramatic now, but it didn't feel that way back then. it just felt like how things were.
so i tried to be good in everyone's eyes. fit in. laugh when they laugh. agree when they agree. nod along even when i had no idea what they were on about. and somewhere in all that fitting in, i think i just… became a version of myself that wasn't really me.
only dead fish go with the flow. i just didn't realise it back then.
i used to care a lot about what people thought of me. like, a lot more than i let on. it's kind of hard to explain but i always had this weird third-person view running in my head.
like — i'm in a conversation with someone, talking normally, but at the exact same time there's this other version of me watching both of us from outside. judging. "was that okay to say?" "should i have worded that differently?" "how do i look right now?"
it's exhausting, honestly. i think too much. still do. i've gotten a little better at catching it, but it never fully goes away.
people around me were fun, no doubt. funny too. i genuinely liked some of them. but most of them didn't really think big. or maybe they did, just about different things. and that gap… it got hard to ignore.
i'd sit there, laughing with them, going along with everything — but in my head i was somewhere else. seeing a bigger picture, or at least trying to. thinking about stuff they weren't thinking about. wondering about things that didn't seem to bother anyone else.
it sounds lonely when i write it like that. it kind of was. and yeah — that meant a lot of fake laughs.
school was… average, i guess. not bad, not great. just there. i had like 2–3 real friends at most. which honestly felt fine most of the time.
till 7th, everything was simple. same place, same people. comfortable in a way that's easy to take for granted.
8th was different. 8th was actually good. not because of marks or anything like that — just because i felt like i actually had people. we had fun. real fun. the kind where you don't check your phone because you're already where you want to be. at least that's what it felt like back then.
funny thing is — now i can barely find more than 2–3 of those guys. people just scatter, i guess.
then 9th hit, and everything just… scattered. new school. new people. i got shifted to a higher section because apparently i was "better in studies." like that was some kind of reward. it didn't feel like one.
that was enough to disconnect everything. honestly? that place felt like hell. i had people to talk to, sure. but no one who'd actually wait for me. no one who'd look up when i walked in late and say "yeah, sit here." no one who actually cared whether i was there or not.
and that difference — between people who are just around and people who actually notice you — you feel it. every single day.
10th just passed. not too fast, not too slow. just… passed. i wasn't a topper. but i wasn't failing either. sat somewhere in the middle and didn't stress too much about it.
75%. not crazy high. not embarrassing either.
and honestly, i don't regret those numbers. because i wasn't just sitting and studying. i was doing calisthenics, trying to make content, figuring out what i was actually into. i was living a little, outside of the marks.
then 11th. i chose PCM. not because i was confident about it or because someone told me to. just because… i wanted to. that felt like enough of a reason at the time.
and yeah. it was bad. barely passed. some subjects were genuinely rough. i won't pretend otherwise.
now i'm in 12th. still no "real" friends. just people i sit next to, talk to in between periods, and then go home. it's all surface level. conversations about nothing. no one really asking how you actually are.
it is what it is. i've kind of made peace with that. doesn't mean it doesn't get to me sometimes though.
something did change though — in may 2026, i went to a hackathon. i didn't have a proper coding background. i honestly wasn't sure what i was doing there.
but just being in that room, watching people build things from scratch in real time — something clicked. like a switch flipped. "wait. we can actually build things? like, real things?"
since then i've been trying to go deeper into it. not just learn syntax — but actually make stuff. build things. figure out what i can do. it's slow. but it feels more real than most things i've done.
at night i think about whether i'll actually get to where i want to go. whether the things i want are actually reachable, or if i'm just telling myself a story.
and the weird part? i don't really feel confused about what i want. i feel like i know what i should be doing. and still — i don't do it. not consistently anyway. that gap between knowing and doing is probably the most frustrating thing about myself right now.
i struggle with sitting down and actually doing the work. not because i can't. when i'm in it, i'm really in it. i just can't seem to start. or stay. and i know that's completely on me. no one else to point at.
i'm trying to fix that. slowly. some days are better than others. trying not to waste whatever potential i think i have. because somewhere i know — if i keep moving like this, i'll look back and regret it. and i really don't want that.
and yeah — one more thing i don't say out loud much: i doubt myself. more than people probably think.
i look at people around me sometimes and just think — wow. they're doing it. they've got it figured out. or at least they look like they do. and i think — i wish i could be like that. that calm, that sure of themselves.
but i'm not. i'm still figuring it out. and maybe that's okay. i don't know yet.

that's not everything…

but it's enough for now.
